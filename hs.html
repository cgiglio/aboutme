High School

Class of 56, not 5600

When I transitioned from Collegiate's Middle School to the High School, I felt cool. It was at this time I started to really prefer the sciences in the classroom and stopped playing any sport other than baseball (I ran track in the winter to stay in shape).

I got to take Advanced Chemistry, Physics, and Math; at the same time, I was usually playing 4 games per weekend in the fall and attending 3 practices a weekend in the winter. Of course, things only ramped up in the spring. During my Junior Year, when I visited numerous schools to attend recruiting camps, I remember writing papers and studying for test in cramped airplane seats or college libraries.

Classes were held in a small building leased to us by a church. In fact, the church recently evicted Collegiate and forced them to buy land and build a whole new building!
